The Most Flooded Area In India

The most flood-prone areas in India are the Brahmaputra and Ganga River basins in the Indo-Gangetic- Brahmaputra plains in North and Northeast India, which carry 60 per cent of the nation's total river flow.Bihar is considered India's most flood-prone state. About 76% of the population in North Bihar lives under the threat of severe flooding. The state is affected by at least five major rivers that originate in Nepal: the Mahananda, Koshi, Bagmati, Burhi Gandak, and Gandak rivers.

The Most Rainy Area In India

Mawsynram receives the highest rainfall in India. It is reportedly the wettest place on Earth, with an average annual rainfall of 11,872 millimetres (467.4 in). According to the Guinness Book of World Records, Mawsynram received 26,000 millimetres (1,000 in) of rainfall in 1985.

The Most Driest Area in India

The North West region of India (Western Rajasthan and Kutchch Region of Gujarat) and part of Ladakh receives less than average rainfall.It is located in the Thar desert. The average temperature reaches 49ºC in summer and 5ºC in winter. Due to the high temperature, the average rainfall is 210 mm which makes Jaisalmer the driest place in India.
